The MS-HCI program at the Georgia Institute of Technology ranks among the top 3 programs in the country.

Due to its popularity, more than half the students admitted into the program come internationally.

Below, we have tried to identify trends and patterns about students being admitted into the program over the last 10 years.

Highlighted below are the countries from which students have been historically enrolled into the MS HCI program.

Out of these countries, we identified that the majority of the students come from three countries: the United States, China, and India.

Here is the distribution of how students from these three countries are distributed into the four tracks - Interactive Computing (IC), Industrial Design (ID), LMC (Literature, Media and Communication), and Psychology (Psych).

To identify why students from these specific countries choose to study abroad, we decided to explore the average annual income across these countries and compare them to the US.

As we can see, most countries students come from have substantially lower average incomes than the US.

Below, we can see the average annual wages across the different American states. Do higher salaries encourage students to study and then work in the US? Does this impact their future career trajectories?

To investigate the impact of higher salaries, we mapped where students end up after completing their degree.

Most students stay in the US, with a few exceptions.

Within the US, popular cities students move to are San Francisco, Washington D.C., Seattle, and New York. Are these due to higher average salaries? Popular industries? Enticing cities?

For the few people that move out of the US. Are most of these returning to their home countries? Will long-term more graduates return home?
